Privacy Policy

Last updated: 01/09/2025

1. About us

iGaming Licensing Limited (“iGL”) is committed to keeping your personal information secure and to adhering to best practice and the principles laid down in data protection regulations in place within the jurisdictions in which we operate.

iGL is registered as a data controller and data processor with the Isle of Man Information Commissioner. This privacy policy explains how iGL will use any personal information that we may collect from you in respect of the services we provide to you.

2. Our Company

iGL is a services provider that offers licensing solutions for operators and software providers across multiple jurisdictions.

3. Why do we need your information?

iGL is obliged to operate in accordance with the framework of the laws and licensing regime in place in the jurisdictions in which it operates together with all applicable international legislation, including financial services, anti-money laundering, taxation, and gambling regulation.

iGL also needs to receive your information to enable us to provide you with the level of services that you have requested.

4. What information do we need?

iGL will collect personal information about you at the beginning of our relationship and may also collect further information from you from time to time to ensure compliance with applicable legislation. We only collect data that is necessary and do not collect special category data such as race, religion, health, or sexual orientation.

5. What we do with it

All personal data we collect is processed by our staff and used in line with the services provided to you and our legal obligations. This may include:

  • Providing proposals and engaging terms of business
  • Providing services to you including administration of client entities
  • Managing assets on your behalf
  • Communicating with third parties such as banks
  • Communicating with Government Departments and regulators
  • Licensing applications
  • Prevention of financial crime and terrorism funding
  • Risk assessments as required under applicable legislation

The way your information is used depends on the services provided. Contact our Data Protection Officer for a specific list relating to your relationship with us.

6. How we store your information

Electronic personal information is stored in secure software on protected servers. Non-electronic personal information is stored in secure premises.

7. Sharing your information

We may share your information with other bodies where lawful, including:

  • Tax and VAT authorities
  • Regulators
  • Law enforcement agencies
  • Third party agencies such as banks
  • Other regulated corporate and trust service providers
  • Third party customer screening entities (e.g., Risk Screen)
  • Software providers and cloud server providers
  • Consultants and/or agents contracted to provide services to iGL

We do not sell, distribute, or lease your personal information without your consent or legal obligation.

8. Transferring your information to other countries

iGL may share your information with organisations in other jurisdictions.

9. How we keep your information accurate

iGL regularly contacts clients to ensure the personal data held is accurate and up to date.

10. What we do with your information when we don’t need it anymore

Your personal information is retained for the minimum period required by law or our retention policy, after which it is securely destroyed.

11. How you can access the information we hold

You can request a copy of your personal information or ask for corrections by contacting our Data Protection Officer at info@igaminglicensing.io.

Data Subject Requests are free of charge, except in cases of excessive requests.

12. iGL’s role as a Data Controller

iGL is a Data Controller and may also act as a Data Processor from time to time.

13. Links to Other Websites

iGL’s website may contain links to other websites. We have no control over their privacy practices and cannot be held responsible for them.

14. Marketing

We only send marketing information with your consent, which you may withdraw at any time.

15. Complaints

If you wish to raise a complaint about how your personal data has been handled, contact our Data Protection Officer. You can also escalate to the Isle of Man Information Commissioner’s Office: ICO Complaint Page.

16. Cookies Policy

Our website uses cookies to improve your experience, track analytics, and manage preferences. This includes:

  • Strictly necessary cookies
  • Performance cookies
  • Functionality cookies
  • Advertising and targeting cookies

We also use Google Analytics cookies. You may opt out by configuring your browser or installing a Google Analytics opt-out plugin.

This policy is subject to change, and updates will be posted on our website.
